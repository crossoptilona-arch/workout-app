Exercise 1: Tandem Stance — 20-30 seconds each foot Stand near a chair. One foot directly in front of the other, heel touching toe. Fix gaze on a point straight ahead — stable gaze dramatically improves balance. Hold 20-30 seconds each foot. Too easy: close eyes for 5-second intervals. Too hard: widen stance slightly. Exercise 2: Single-Leg Stand — 20-30 seconds each side Fingertips on chair only. Lift one foot slightly off the floor. Fix your gaze. Hold 20-30 seconds. Switch. Progression: two-finger → one-finger → hover → no touch. Move only when current level feels controlled. Exercise 3: Weight Shifts — 10 reps each direction Hands lightly on chair. Slowly shift weight to right foot until left foot lightens. Hold 3 seconds. Shift left. Repeat. Progress to actually lifting the unloaded foot 1-2 inches off the ground. Safety rule: Always have a chair, wall, or counter within one arm's reach. Touch support the instant you feel unsteady. There is no benefit to falling.
